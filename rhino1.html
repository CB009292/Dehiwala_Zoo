Rhinoceros

Rhinoceroses are big herbivorous animals with horned snouts that distinguish them. Rhinoceros is derived from the Greek words "rhino" (nose) and "ceros" (horn). Rhinos come in five species and 11 subspecies, with some having two horns and others having only one. Rhinos have been driven close to extinction because their horns are employed in traditional medicine for their alleged curative abilities.

Horn Value

Rhinos are illegally hunted for their horns, which on the black market sell for the same weight-for-weight price as gold. Their horns are occasionally sold as trophies or ornaments, but they are more commonly ground up and utilized in traditional Chinese medicine.

Speed

Despite their enormous size, rhinos can run at speeds of up to 30 miles per hour (48 kph).

Skin

Rhinos have a thick, strong skin that is around 0.6 in (1.5 cm) thick, which is why they are often called "pachyderms."

Rhino Horns

Rhino horns may grow up to 5 feet (1.5 meters) long and increase as much as 3 inches (8 cm) every year.
